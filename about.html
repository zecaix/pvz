Plants Vs Zombies What is it?

Plants vs. Zombies (abbreviated as PvZ) is a tower defense video game developed and originally published by PopCap Games and it is the first installment in the Plants vs. Zombies series. The game involves homeowners who use a variety of different plants to prevent waves of zombies from entering their houses and "eating their brains". It follows the affiliates of David "Crazy Dave" Blazing as they use his plants to defend against a zombie invasion, led by Dr. Edgar George Zomboss. Plants vs. Zombies is a video game where players strategically place different plants and fungi around a house to prevent zombies from reaching it. The game has different levels and layouts, including nighttime and rooftop levels, with various types of zombies that have different attributes. The player must gather "sun" to plant and purchase upgrades for their plants. If a zombie reaches the player's house, the game ends with a message saying "The zombies ate your brains!"

Plants Vs Zombies History

It was first going to release on October 28, 2008, back when it was titled Bloom & Doom, but as an early version, and it got delayed. It was first released on May 5, 2009. The game received a positive response from critics and was nominated for multiple Interactive Achievement Awards, alongside receiving praise for its musical score. The game is followed by a sequel, Plants vs. Zombies 2, which was released on July 9, 2013, in Australia and New Zealand, then it was released worldwide on August 15, 2013.
Plants vs. Zombies is a game that uses many cultural references in its names of stages and mini-games. The gravestones' inscriptions and several levels in the game reference Monty Python, Harry Potter, Isaac Asimov's I, Robot, Led Zeppelin, and gaming memes. The dancing zombie originally resembled Michael Jackson, but it was replaced with a disco-dancing zombie after objections from Michael Jackson's estate. The game was originally planned to be called Lawn of the Dead, but George A. Romero didn't give permission to use the name. Plants vs. Zombies has been released on multiple platforms and has been referenced in other games as well.

Overall It's a delightful game that is both addictive and accessible, and you'll never look at your garden the same way again.
